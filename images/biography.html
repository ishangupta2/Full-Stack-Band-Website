Twenty One Pilots

The American musical sensation, Twenty One Pilots was formed by college friends, Tyler Joseph alongside former members Nick Thomas and Chris Salih in Columbus, Ohio.

After spurring a an enormous grassroot regional following across Ohio and the Midwest through constant and energetic touring and an astute use of social media, Twenty One Pilots had their first major mainstream advancement with their 2015 multi-platinum album, Blurryface. Featuring the Grammy-winning single "Stressed Out" and its follow-up chart hit "Ride," the album became one of the year's best-selling albums in the world. The trio self-released their eponymous debut album of rap-infused, high-tech pop in December 2009 before Thomas and Salih left the band in 2011.

Afterward, Tyler Joesph recruited Josh Dun as a replacement drummer and Twenty One Pilots continued as a duo. By working with Mark Eshelman to expand their audience with a series of videos, the band’s growth drew the attention of major labels. In 2012, they signed to Atlantic supplementary Fueled by Ramen, releasing the Three Songs EP. On January 2013, Vessel was released, featuring platinum singles "Holding on to You," "House of Gold," and "Car Radio.
